Projects: Risk Assessment

Management Testing Scenarios

The goal of risk analysis is to qualitatively or quantitatively determine the likelihood that an ecosystem indicator will reach or remain in an undesirable state (i.e., breach a reference limit), over a specified time horizon.

Risk analyses link ecosystem components to human activities or natural phenomena that occur on land (e.g., coastal development, agriculture, changing river flows, etc.), in the air (e.g., weather, climate), and in the ocean itself (e.g., shipping, naval exercises, energy extraction, physical and chemical conditions). These links often involve comparisons between the sensitivity of the component to exposure by these activities or phenomena (see Risk Figure, at left). These analyses can be used to identify fruitful avenues for testing management scenarios.

A number of analytical techniques can be used for ecosystem-scale risk analyses, ranging from qualitative to fully quantitative models. Risk analyses explicitly consider the inevitable uncertainties involved in understanding and quantifying ecosystem dynamics and their positive and negative impacts on social systems.

Common themes for risk analyses include climate scenarios, extractive activities like fishing, and coastal land uses that cause pollution.
